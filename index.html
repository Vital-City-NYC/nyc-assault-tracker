How we did this

This dashboard is built to be fully auditable. Every number on this page can be reproduced by replaying the queries below against the NYPD's public datasets.

Data sources

  • NYPD Complaint Data — Historic (NYC Open Data, dataset qgea-i56i). Covers 2006 through the end of the prior calendar year. Dataset page.
  • NYPD Complaint Data — Current (Year To Date) (NYC Open Data, dataset 5uac-w243). Covers all complete quarters of the current reporting year. Dataset page.
  • NYPD Seven Major Felony Offenses (1993–2024) — published historical table used for pre-2006 felony assault annual totals. Historical table PDF.
  • NYPD CompStat weekly reports — not used for the live numbers here (they are PDF-only and are not machine-readable) but are the authoritative source for week-level NYPD crime reporting. Any discrepancy between CompStat and this dashboard reflects classification or reconciliation adjustments made by NYPD after initial reporting.

What counts as an assault

The NYPD classifies assault complaints into two buckets based on the state Penal Law:

  • Felony assault — offense code FELONY ASSAULT, law category FELONY. Corresponds to Penal Law Article 120 first- and second-degree assault, and certain aggravated or weapons-related third-degree counts. This is one of the NYPD's seven major felonies tracked continuously since 1993.
  • Misdemeanor assault — offense code ASSAULT 3 & RELATED OFFENSES, law category MISDEMEANOR. Corresponds primarily to Penal Law §120.00 third-degree assault and related counts. The NYPD did not systematically publish machine-readable misdemeanor complaint data before 2006, so 1993 and 2000 baselines for misdemeanor assault are not shown.

How the numbers are calculated

On page load, the dashboard issues these Socrata SoQL queries live from your browser to NYC Open Data:

  • Monthly counts, 2019–2024, from qgea-i56i, grouped by date_trunc_ym(cmplnt_fr_dt) and law_cat_cd, filtered to the two assault offense codes above.
  • Monthly counts, 2025 forward, from 5uac-w243, using the same grouping.
  • Borough breakdown, latest full year, from 5uac-w243, grouped by boro_nm and law_cat_cd.
  • Annual counts, 2006 onward, from qgea-i56i (plus 2025 from 5uac-w243), grouped by date_extract_y(cmplnt_fr_dt).

Percent change is computed as (later − earlier) ÷ earlier × 100, rounded to one decimal place. All counts are based on the date of occurrence (cmplnt_fr_dt), not the date of report.

Baseline years

The baselines in the headline comparison chart were chosen to mark reference points the public tends to remember:

  • 1993 — the year before CompStat launched and the beginning of NYPD's continuous seven-major-felony tracking. Close to the modern peak of felony assault.
  • 2000 — the end of the 1990s crime decline, roughly the midpoint of Giuliani's second term.
  • 2010 — a low point in NYC felony assault during the Bloomberg era.
  • 2019 — the last full year before the COVID-19 pandemic, commonly used as a "pre-pandemic" benchmark.

Pre-2006 felony assault annual totals come from the NYPD's published historical crime tables. These numbers are rounded, reconciled figures the NYPD reports for each year, and may differ from a raw complaint-level count by ±1 to 2% due to late reclassifications, duplicate removal, or other post-publication adjustments. The complaint-level open data used here (2006 onward) can be re-queried at any time.

Known limitations

  • Reported crimes only. These datasets include only crimes reported to the NYPD. National victimization surveys consistently find that roughly 40 to 50 percent of violent assaults go unreported.
  • Classification drift. What the NYPD files as "felony" vs. "misdemeanor" assault depends on officer judgment at the complaint stage. Classification practices can shift over time and across precincts.
  • Data revision lag. The Current (YTD) dataset is updated on a roughly quarterly cadence. The most recent three to six months of data will typically move upward slightly as late reports are filed and processed.
  • Methodology break at 2006. Pre-2006 totals come from NYPD's published annual tables; 2006 onward comes from complaint-level open data. The two match within a few percent but are not identical.
  • Not per-capita. Counts on this page are absolute — they do not adjust for changes in the city's population. NYC's population grew from about 7.3 million in 1993 to about 8.3 million in 2024 (roughly 14 percent), so per-capita declines are somewhat steeper than raw count declines.
  • Boroughs only. Precinct- and neighborhood-level breakdowns are available in the same datasets; they are out of scope for this top-line dashboard.

Reproducing these numbers

All live queries hit data.cityofnewyork.us and are viewable in your browser's network tab. A representative query for the 2025 felony count:

GET https://data.cityofnewyork.us/resource/5uac-w243.json?$query=SELECT count(*) WHERE ofns_desc='FELONY ASSAULT' AND law_cat_cd='FELONY' AND date_extract_y(cmplnt_fr_dt)=2025

No data is stored on this page; every count is recomputed each time you load it.
